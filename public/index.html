Queer Film Club

A safe space for LGBTQ+ people to enjoy film

The Queer Film Club will be kicking off with The Birdcage on October 17th! Join us as we watch queer films, discuss issues impacting the LGBTQ+ community, and build community with queer folks in Denver!

The Birdcage

Engaged couple Val Goldman and Barbara Keeley introduce their future in-laws. Val's father, Armand, a gay Miami drag club owner, pretends to be straight and attempts to hide his relationship with Albert, his life partner and the club's flamboyant star, so as to please Barbara's father, controversial Republican Sen. Kevin Keeley.

But I'm A Cheerleader

Megan considers herself a typical American girl. She excels in school and cheerleading, and she has a handsome football-playing boyfriend, even though she isn't crazy for him. So she's stunned when her parents decide she's gay and send her to True Directions, a camp meant to alter her sexual orientation. While there, Megan meets a teen lesbian, Graham. Though Megan still feels confused, she starts to have feelings for Graham.

Paris Is Burning

This documentary focuses on drag queens living in New York City and their "house" culture, which provides a sense of community and support for the flamboyant and often socially shunned performers. Groups from each house compete in elaborate balls inspired by the world of fashion. Touching on issues of racism and poverty, the film features interviews with a number of renowned queens, including Willi Ninja, Pepper LaBeija and Dorian Corey.
